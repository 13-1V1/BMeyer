Network & Data Center

The infrastructure behind "the cloud." This coursework pulled back the curtain on how modern data centers are designed, built, and kept running.

NETECH-113 · Data Center

How data centers really work

Studied the core components of a facility — servers, storage arrays, and network gear — plus the infrastructure that supports them: power distribution units, UPS systems, generators, cooling, and physical security.

  • Design considerations like hot-aisle/cold-aisle layouts, airflow containment, raised floors, and power usage effectiveness (PUE).
  • Operations: inventory control, environmental monitoring (temperature, humidity, power draw), and maintenance workflows.
  • Redundancy and high availability — backup power, network failover, and how virtualization and containerization pack many services onto shared hardware.
