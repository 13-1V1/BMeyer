MFGT‑100 Industrial Safety

Safety is paramount in any shop or lab environment. This class used interactive Amatrol eLearning modules to teach the basic principles for keeping workplaces accident‑free. The training covered common causes of accidents, the role of OSHA, and personal safety actions such as properly selecting and using personal protective equipment (PPE), communicating hazards, and locking out equipment during maintenance【237728176636443†L61-L66】.

  • Developed an understanding of safety policies, the consequences of unsafe behaviour, and how to build a strong safety culture. We learned how OSHA and the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) set standards for general workplace safety, material handling, tools, and machinery【237728176636443†L98-L105】.
  • Completed modules on Personal Protective Equipment that taught how to identify hazards and choose appropriate protective gear for the head, eyes, ears, hands and body, and how to use PPE correctly【237728176636443†L113-L117】.
  • Studied Hazard Communication, learning about chemical and biological hazards, how to read safety data sheets, and the importance of properly labeling containers and communicating risks to others【237728176636443†L125-L131】.
  • Practiced Lockout/Tagout procedures, including the identification of hazardous energy sources, applying LOTO devices and tags, and understanding the energy‑control programs needed before servicing equipment【237728176636443†L139-L143】.
  • Discussed ergonomics and workplace design to reduce fatigue and injury, as well as emergency response planning.

MFGT‑113 Machining Fundamentals

This course introduced the fundamentals of machining and metal fabrication. We learned to interpret engineering drawings and perform machine shop math, then applied that knowledge on the shop floor to cut and shape parts. The program also included OSHA‑10 certification and hands‑on experience with a range of machine tools【569410679499134†L117-L123】.

  • Gained proficiency using lathes, milling machines and drill presses to drill, tap and ream holes and to turn and mill cylindrical and flat parts to specification【569410679499134†L117-L123】.
  • Learned to read and interpret blueprints and develop machine shop math skills for calculating feeds, speeds and tolerances. We measured parts using micrometers, calipers and other precision instruments.
  • Practiced selecting appropriate tooling and cutting parameters for different materials and gained exposure to the basics of computer numerical control (CNC) machining【569410679499134†L117-L123】.

Integration with Other Disciplines

The skills gained in manufacturing technology complement my work in welding and software. Understanding how parts are machined helps me design better assemblies and appreciate the constraints of physical manufacturing when writing control software for robots and CNC machines.
