Automotive

Understanding what makes an engine tick. AUTECH-112 covered engine fundamentals for technicians — the systems, fluids, and failure modes behind how a modern engine runs.

Engine design

Interference vs. non-interference engines

Wrote on the trade-off between the two designs. In an interference engine, pistons and valves share the same space at different times — so a failed timing belt can bend valves or crack a head, but the design allows higher compression, more power, and better efficiency. A non-interference engine keeps them clear, so a broken belt usually just stops the engine without damage — safer and cheaper to fix, but with some performance sacrificed.

Fluids

Why engine oil and coolant degrade

Explored how the fluids that protect an engine break down over time: oil oxidizes under heat and combustion contaminants, turning acidic and forming sludge that raises wear; coolant loses its corrosion inhibitors, drops in pH, and can even drive electrolysis that eats away at metal components. It's a good reminder of why maintenance intervals matter.
