Biography

For most of my life I chased variety. I worked a long list of jobs and picked up a little of everything, but I never stayed in one place for long — I was always restless for whatever came next. That restlessness eventually turned into a harder struggle with addiction, and in December 2024 I made the most important decision of my life: I entered rehab and spent six months getting sober and rebuilding from the ground up.

When I finished, I started over completely. At 38, living with my mom, I enrolled at College of the Canyons — and everything changed. I went from battling hard drugs to a full-time, straight-A student who genuinely loves the work. I found a real passion for machining, welding, and technology, and the drive that once pulled me in every direction finally had somewhere meaningful to go.

That gratitude showed in how I approached school, and it didn't go unnoticed. My instructors offered me a role as a student teaching assistant and lab technician for the welding and manufacturing department — a student and an employee at the same time. I'm more focused and ambitious than I've ever been, and I'm just getting started.
