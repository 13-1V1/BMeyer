Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)

My SMAW coursework took me from striking my first arc through mastering all‑position welds. Over three sequential classes I practiced flat, horizontal, vertical and overhead groove and fillet welds, learned to choose electrodes and settings for different metals, and prepared weld coupons for inspection. This sequence culminated in hands‑on tests and NC3 certification.

  • WELD‑101A Intro to SMAW: Covered equipment setup and safety, electrode types and polarity, and produced flat‑position stringer beads and fillet welds.
  • WELD‑101B Intermediate SMAW: Built on fundamentals with horizontal and vertical groove welds, emphasising arc length, travel speed and heat control.
  • WELD‑101C Advanced SMAW: Introduced overhead and multi‑pass welding, with a focus on identifying and correcting common defects. Completed test coupons for certification and learned about weld codes and inspection.

Metal Fabrication & TIG Welding

Beyond arc welding I began exploring the broader world of metal fabrication and precision TIG welding. These introductory courses taught me how to read blueprints, lay out materials and use shop tools to cut and assemble parts. In TIG lab sessions I worked on controlling the torch, adding filler rod and maintaining clean, precise welds on mild steel and aluminium. I plan to refine these skills in future semesters.

  • WELD‑100A Intro to Metal Fabrication: An overview of fabrication tools and techniques. Projects included simple fixtures and frames cut with saws and formed with grinders and drills. Emphasis was on layout, measurement and safe use of equipment.
  • WELD‑112A Intro to Gas Tung Arc Welding (TIG): Focused on establishing a stable arc, adding filler rod and controlling heat input. Practiced TIG welds on coupons to develop coordination and puddle control.

Skills & Safety

Welding is as much about preparation and safety as it is about puddle control. These classes instilled habits that carry over into every project.

  • Setting up welding machines correctly for different electrodes and metals.
  • Preparing joints and fit‑up to ensure strong, consistent welds.
  • Inspecting welds visually and destructively to identify defects and improve technique.
  • Following shop discipline and PPE protocols to protect yourself and others.

Certifications & Next Steps
