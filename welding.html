Background

Prior to having proper instruction, I owned an FCAW machine with which I would create and repair small projects at home. Being self-taught at the time, these welds were not the prettiest—but I am proud to say that none of my welds from then have ever broken. I have always enjoyed working with my hands and welding in general, so in the Summer of 2025 I enrolled in an SMAW course at College of the Canyons.

Skills and Techniques

During my SMAW course, I gained hands-on experience with:

  • Electrodes: Focused on 6013, 6010, and extensively practiced with 7018 for high-strength, low-hydrogen welds.
  • Beading Techniques: Mastered standard beads, whip and pause for penetration control, weave beads for wide coverage, V-beads for groove welds, and more.
  • Positions & Materials: Worked on projects with material thicknesses from 10 gauge to 3/4" steel in all welding positions—flat, horizontal, vertical, and overhead.
  • Inspection & Quality Control: Learned visual inspection methods and destructive testing such as bend tests to ensure structural integrity.
  • Troubleshooting: Developed the ability to diagnose machine issues and correct weld defects such as porosity, slag inclusions, and undercut.
  • Plans & Terminology: Knowledge of and ability to read WPS, Welding Symbols, and blueprints with an understanding of concepts and math calculations.

Equipment & Tools

My training has included the use of a variety of welding machines and tools:

  • Lincoln Electric SMAW welders for class projects
  • Angle grinders for material preparation and cleanup
  • CAG-A, PAC, OFC for weld preparation
  • Clamps, jigs, and fixtures for secure work positioning

Safety & Best Practices

Safety is an essential part of my welding education ensuring:

  • Knowledge and understanding of SDS and OSHA standards
  • Proper PPE usage: welding helmet, safety glasses, gloves, and flame-resistant clothing
  • Ventilation procedures to avoid inhaling fumes
  • Safe electrode handling and machine shut-down protocols
  • Work area organization to prevent tripping or fire hazards
