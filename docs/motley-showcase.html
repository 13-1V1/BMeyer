01

Why it's different

In a normal collector you love your Charizard because you earned it — but everyone has the same 151. Here you love your creature because you made it exist, and no one else has it. Authorship is the hook; the essences you fuse drive both how it looks and how it fights, so collecting, breeding, and team-building collapse into one act: combining ideas.

03

The systems

Every one of these is built and unit-tested.

Combine, don't inflate

Every creature shares one stat budget — fuse more essences and it gets wilder, not stronger. Stacking is a trade-off, never a cheat.

Momentum

Exploiting a weakness earns tempo toward an extra action, so a small type chart plays deep — a good sequence snowballs.

Synergy

Certain pairings unlock combos — Overload builds Momentum faster, Skyborne always acts first. Which essences you fuse shapes the fight.

Moves

Each creature picks from a movepool every turn — hit hard, or trade damage to apply a status. Battle is a series of decisions.

Hidden types

Rare Wild creatures sit off the triangle — glass cannons with no weakness, but they take double status damage, so status hard-counters them.

Grow forever

Creatures never die and level infinitely — but gains are asymptotic, so a clever low-level team still beats a grinder. Bloom to prestige.
